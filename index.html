Fear & Greed Index

Understanding the Fear & Greed Index

The Fear & Greed Index is a tool developed by CNN Business to measure two primary market emotions that influence how much investors are willing to pay for stocks: fear and greed. It is calculated based on seven equally weighted indicators:

  • 0-24: Extreme Fear - Investors are selling off stocks, driving prices down.
  • 25-44: Fear - Investors are hesitant to buy, and prices may be stagnating or falling.
  • 45-55: Neutral - The market is balanced, with no strong bias towards fear or greed.
  • 56-75: Greed - Investors are eager to buy, pushing prices higher.
  • 76-100: Extreme Greed - Investors are aggressively buying, potentially leading to overvalued assets.

A higher number indicates more greed, while a lower number indicates more fear. Understanding these emotions can help investors make more informed decisions.
